About Me

    My name is Zak Breitenstein. I'm currently 22 years old, and I'm a junior at Utah Tech University in the Software Engineering program. I originally began attending Dixie State University back in Fall of 2019, which was when I took the original Web Design class. In 2020, I took a break after the Fall semester because I was struggling to get things done with the online schooling format. I'd just lost all my motivation and wanted to try doing something else, so I got a job as a fuel attendant at Fabulous Freddy's (Image above is me in uniform). After a little while I decided to go back, and now I'm doing better than ever before.

    I originally got into programming at around age 12. I was learning simple, pretty niche scripting languages that were native to programs I liked to use to create games. I really enjoyed seeing the code I created do things on screen. I felt like I could do anything I wanted, and for the most part, that's still the way that I see programming. I think it's a lot of fun to make applications, although often challenging. Once I got older, I started becoming more serious about programming and software engineering which led to me becoming a Computer Science major at Dixie. I ended up changing majors to Software Engineering and that's where I'm currently at now.

    Outside of programming, I'm also really into music. I play the guitar, and I also occasionally compose my own songs. It's a fun hobby, but it's not something I'm really all that serious about. I also skateboard from time to time and go running or jogging late at night. I've put a decent amount of time into learning 3D modelling and graphic design on my own time, mostly for the sake of making games, but I still have a lot to learn. I love learning how to do a bunch of different things. I'm definitely not great at a lot of things, but I would say I'm good at picking up new skills quickly. That's why I'd say that adaptability is my greatest strength.

    In the future, I'm obviously looking to become a Software Engineer. I don't really have any idea what sort of company I'd like to end up at. I have some connections to people that are currently working in the industry, but they come from very different backgrounds to me. I don't know where exactly I fit in, but I'm looking forward to hopefully finding out. Until then I'm just trying to enjoy the ride.
